Freediving for Kids

Kids meet the water — breath, play, and calm.

Freediving gives a child room to grow. Steady breath, a strong body, a calm mind — learned in play, in the water, at their own pace. Water comfort comes first, taught by Molchanovs-certified instructors — the same lineage the whole school comes from.

Little kids

Play and water comfort. Floating, splashing, breath games — feeling at home in the water is the whole lesson.

Older kids

Breath and technique, still learned in play: longer glides, cleaner dives, calm before each one.

16–17

The full Molchanovs Wave 1 course — real depth, real certification, with parental consent.

For parents

Can I watch — can I be in the water?

Both. You stay close — on the deck, or in the water on your own buoy.

How many kids per instructor?

One. Your child has the instructor's full attention for the whole session.

At what age can a child start freediving?

Water comfort starts as young as a child is happy to play in the pool — every session runs at their pace. At 16–17 the full Molchanovs Wave 1 course opens up, with parental consent.

What if my child stays at the water's edge?

Then that's where the session is. It starts wherever your child is that day — feet on the steps, waist-deep, already swimming — and moves at their pace. Each step into the water is theirs, and that's what makes it stick.

What does a session look like for a 5-year-old vs a teenager?

For a five-year-old: floating, splashing, breath games — water comfort is the whole lesson. For a teenager: technique, longer glides, real depth when they're ready. The age bands above walk through it. Either way, attention sets the length: the session runs as long as your child's interest does.

Can my child get a freediving certification?

Yes — from 16, with parental consent, teens take the full Molchanovs Wave 1 course and leave certified freedivers. Until then play leads, and certification follows when the child wants it.

Is breath-holding safe for a developing child?

Kids' sessions stay shallow and playful — one instructor, one child, always within arm's reach. Breath games are short and easy, and there are no numbers to chase: the session follows your child's comfort, and comfort is the measure.

Comfort in the water is where a child begins. Before any skill, they get to simply be in it: unhurried, at their own pace, with someone they trust right there beside them. That ease is the whole point.

A child who feels at home in the water carries that calm back out of it, into the rest of their day.
