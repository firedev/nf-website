The practice

Freediving trains life.

The water makes attention physical. What happens between a body signal and your reaction is the real training.

Meditation with honest feedback

In ordinary meditation, I can sit, think, and call it practice. Underwater the lie becomes expensive.

Tense up — spend oxygen. Believe every alarm — turn early. Start arguing with the body — the body wins.

The best dive begins when the inner subtitles stop. There is water, pressure, the line, the body and the moment. Less language. More reality.

The mental ladder

From one point to the whole field.

Molchanovs builds the mental side wave by wave. The names get more advanced. The practice stays human.

Wave 1

Body scan & visualization

Find tension without fighting it. Rehearse a realistic dive until the route feels known.

Wave 2

Mindfulness

Notice breath, heartbeat, contractions and thought without turning every signal into a command.

Wave 3

Attention deconcentration

Widen attention from a point into a field. See the periphery. Hear the whole soundscape. Feel the body at once.

Wave 4

Pressure practice

Small focuses, self-talk and a repeatable start ritual keep attention available when the stakes rise.

Message Nick

Breath-hold safety is not a mental trick.

Never train in water alone and never hyperventilate before a dive. Every in-water session uses an active, qualified buddy, conservative limits and a clear decision to surface with reserve.
