Freediving for Adults

A way back to yourself — your own buoy, your own pace.

Just breath, body, and attention. We teach the steadiness of the breath-hold — it follows you back into your day, into whatever the day holds. Start where you are; the water meets you there. Molchanovs Wave 1 through 3, off Phuket.

The urge to breathe is a reflex — and once you feel that for yourself, the whole thing opens up. You stay with it, ease arrives on its own, and the line becomes somewhere you want to be.

Your body already knows how. Put your face in the water and the heart slows down by itself — the mammalian dive reflex, a calm the body has carried all along. Freediving is learning to rest inside it.

Any point on the line is a complete dive — the day's depth is yours to set. That ease is what you take back up. You come up more alive, and it follows you.

Wave 1 · 12–20 m

Your first real dives. Theory, pool and open water: breath-holds, equalization, safety, the line. The one prerequisite is a swim test.

You leave a certified freediver, at home in open water.

Wave 2 · 24–30 m

Frenzel equalization and your first freefall — the part of the dive where the water carries you. For Wave 1 divers and crossovers from other agencies.

You leave with clean technique and the freefall in your body.

Wave 3 · 34–40 m

Mouthfill equalization and long, quiet freefalls — ease at depth, staying soft and clear where the light turns blue.

You leave with mastery that feels like rest.

Every Wave ends with a Molchanovs certification recognized worldwide — and lifetime access to Base Training, structured practice for the time between courses.

Your first session

Your first dives are warm-ups — short, easy, well within what your body can do.

Your instructor is right beside you, dive after dive.

You surface whenever you choose. The line waits.

Before we start, a short medical questionnaire — so we know your body and can look after you properly in the water.

We run two rhythms off Phuket: deep water on Saturdays — off the boat, down the line — and pool sessions on Sundays for technique and breath-work. One person per buoy, always your own. Come to either, or both.
