Let’s freedive

Why freediving?

Meditation with honest feedback.

A dive gives clear signals: relaxation, pressure, equalization, buoyancy, the urge to breathe, the turn and the recovery breath.

Training is simple. Notice the signal. Relax what can relax. Equalize early. Stay with the line and the buddy. Surface with reserve.

Depth is feedback. A good dive ends with calm breathing on the surface.

What I teach

The advanced work starts early.

Molchanovs introduces its deeper attention work across Wave 3 and Wave 4. I bring the same principles into ordinary training from the beginning: in plain language, through the body.

Notice

Read the body.

Use body scan and mindfulness before the dive. Notice tension, heartbeat, thoughts and the urge to breathe.

Widen

Make attention a field.

Attention deconcentration spreads awareness across sight, sound and the whole body. Keep more than one signal in attention.

Choose

Do the next small thing.

Equalize. Soften the jaw. Follow the line. Turn on the plan. Finish with recovery breathing.

I currently teach official Molchanovs courses through Wave 2. Advanced attention practice is separate and does not issue Wave 3 or Wave 4 certification.

Why nofins?

No-fins uses the body directly.

In the water, no-fins means moving with your own body. The line, lanyard and buddy stay close, so the practice can stay calm and safe.

On land, the same training is useful: notice the signal, relax the body, choose the next clear action.
